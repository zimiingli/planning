\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

DIAL's weakest result is on FEVER, where all methods incur high
cost due to the environment's extreme Type~I structure: DIAL costs
16.51$\times$base and achieves 49.8\% SR, while cheaper alternatives
like SEAG (47.3\% at 17.19$\times$) are only marginally lower in both
SR and cost. Rollout value in FEVER concentrates in the first one
or two steps, and random exploration misses these critical steps with
50\% probability. This failure mode is predicted by the two-source
model: FEVER is the most extreme Type~I environment, where undirected
exploration is least efficient, and exposes the cost of insisting on
signal-agnostic exploration in high-$p_I$ regimes. Addressing this
through curriculum-based exploration is an important direction for
future work. We verify our findings on three backbones (4B--8B) across
all six environments (Appendix~\ref{app:multi-backbone}); testing on
larger models (70B+) remains future work. The two-source model assumes
linear conditional relationships and serves as an explanatory
framework rather than a precise predictor of $\rho$ values. Finally,
DIAL requires $N_{\text{explore}}{=}50$ exploration episodes per new
(environment, backbone) deployment---a modest but non-zero one-time
cost amortized over subsequent inference, and the only cost incurred
beyond the per-step inference budget reported in
\S\ref{sec:method}.
