\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

EAAG's weakest result is on FEVER, where all methods incur high
cost due to the environment's extreme Type~I structure: EAAG costs
16.51$\times$base and achieves 49.8\% SR, while cheaper alternatives
like SEAG (47.3\% at 17.19$\times$) are only marginally lower in both
SR and cost. Rollout value in FEVER concentrates in the first one
or two steps, and random exploration misses these critical steps with
50\% probability. This failure mode is predicted by the Two-Source
Model: FEVER is the most extreme Type~I environment, where undirected
exploration is least efficient. Addressing this through
curriculum-based exploration is an important direction for future work. We verify our findings on three backbones (4B--8B)
across all six environments
(Appendix~\ref{app:multi-backbone}); testing on larger models (70B+)
remains future work. The Two-Source Model assumes linear conditional
relationships and serves as an explanatory framework rather than a
precise predictor of $\rho$ values. EAAG requires 50 exploration
episodes per new environment, a modest but non-zero cost amortized
over subsequent deployment.
