\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

FEVER is DIAL's weakest setting: useful rollouts concentrate in the earliest steps, which random exploration may miss, and on one backbone a fixed-direction baseline matches or slightly exceeds DIAL on the SR-cost frontier. Curriculum-based exploration is a natural extension. DIAL incurs a one-time exploration cost per (environment, backbone), amortized over subsequent inference; in exchange, it removes the need for manual direction tuning. We exclude end-to-end RL gating controllers, whose reward-cost objective and online training can confound direction-attribution.
