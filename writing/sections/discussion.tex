\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

EAAG underperforms on FEVER, where rollout value concentrates in the
first one or two steps and random exploration misses these critical
steps with 50\% probability. This failure mode is itself predicted by
the Two-Source Model: FEVER is the most extreme Type~I environment,
where undirected exploration is least efficient. Addressing this
through curriculum-based exploration is an important direction for
future work. We verify our findings on three backbones (4B--8B)
across all eight environments
(Appendix~\ref{app:multi-backbone}); testing on larger models (70B+)
remains future work. The Two-Source Model assumes linear conditional
relationships and serves as an explanatory framework rather than a
precise predictor of $\rho$ values. EAAG requires 50 exploration
episodes per new environment, a modest but non-zero cost amortized
over subsequent deployment.
