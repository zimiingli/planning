\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}
\vspace{-0.1in}
DIAL's weakest result is on FEVER: although DIAL still
beats every baseline (49.8\% SR at 16.51$\times$ base),
the absolute cost remains high because rollout value
concentrates in the first one or two steps and random
exploration misses them with 50\% probability.
Curriculum-based exploration in high-$p_I$ regimes is a
natural extension. DIAL also incurs a one-time exploration cost per
(environment, backbone) deployment, amortized over
subsequent inference. In exchange, it eliminates the
manual direction tuning that fixed-direction methods
require.
